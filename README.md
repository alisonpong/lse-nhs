## National Health Service (NHS) Appointment Analysis
### LSE Data Analytics Accelerator Course Project
---
#### 🏥 Project Overview
The National Health Service (NHS) must plan future infrastructure and resources to keep pace with a growing population. To budget effectively, it needs a clear understanding of how services are currently used across its network and where pressure on the system is greatest. One significant concern is the cost incurred when patients miss their general practice (GP) appointments.
1)	How are appointment volumes and wasted capacity from missed appointments distributed across regions and services, and which regions are most affected?
2)	What appointment features – such as mode, service type, and lead time – are associated with higher no show risk?
3)	Where should the NHS prioritise interventions and capacity?

This project performs a data-driven diagnostic analysis using Python to evaluate factors contributing to appointment volume distributions, profile the risk variables associated with patient no-shows, and provide actionable evidence to prioritize future capacity and targeted clinical interventions.
